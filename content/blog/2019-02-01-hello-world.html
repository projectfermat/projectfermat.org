Welcome to Project Fermat.

This community was formed to promote discussion and collaboration between people who love mathematics but are not necessarily mathematicians. The discussions in this community mostly hover around mathematics, computer science, and algorithms. If you find these topics interesting, you are welcome to join us and be a part of our community. You are also welcome to invite your friends and colleagues and help this community grow.

Apart from the group mailing list, there are Slack and IRC channels for the community as well. To learn more about the other channels, please see: Join Project Fermat.

It has been about two weeks since the group mailing list was created. We have already had an interesting discussion about periodic functions initiated by our community member, Phillip B. He posted a problem which involved finding two periodic functions \( f \) and \( g \) from \( \mathbb{R} \) to \( \mathbb{R} \) such that their sum \( f + g \) is the identity function. The solution involves treating the set of real numbers \( \mathbb{R} \) as a vector space over the set of rational numbers \( \mathbb{Q} \). See this discussion for more details.

We hope to have more such interesting discussions in future. We have the following goals in mind when we have such discussions:

  1. Be a platform where students and professionals of all backgrounds can come together to learn and discuss mathematics.
  2. Welcome discussion on related areas such as computer science, algorithms, cryptography, related software tools such as \( \LaTeX \), NumPy, etc.
  3. Discuss mathematical puzzles for fun!

We will be posting regular updates about the activities of our community on our Twitter profile, so please follow us there if you would would like to receive our updates.
