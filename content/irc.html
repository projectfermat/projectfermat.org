Project Fermat IRC User Guide

There is a channel for the Project Fermat community at the Freenode IRC network. Some members of the community hang out in this channel. This is an open channel and anyone interested in mathematics, computer science, and algorithms are welcome to join this channel and be a part of the community.

This guide describes the connection details, an example of how to connect and some nifty commands for IRC users and operators.

Connection Details

Use the following connection details to connect to our channel.

  • Network: chat.freenode.net
  • Channel: #projectfermat

The above connection details are required while connecting to our channel via Webchat or an IRC client. The next two sections elaborate this further. While there are many IRC clients available on the web, we choose Pidgin to demonstrate how to connect to our channel.

Webchat

This method of connecting via the web based interface does not require the installation of any additional tools. This is the simplest method to connect to our channel.

  1. Visit https://webchat.freenode.net/?channels=projectfermat with your web browser.
  2. Enter a nickname of your choice.
  3. If you are new to the Freenode network, you would not have your nickname registered with the network, so ignore this point. If you have registered your nickname before, and you want to connect using your password, select Auth to Services, set Username to your nickname and Password to your password.
  4. Click Connect.

Pidgin

There are many IRC clients available on the web. For the sake of brevity, we want to demonstrate how to connect to our channel with only one of them. We choose Pidgin as the client here.

  1. Download Pidgin from https://pidgin.im/ and install it.
    • If you are on Mac and use Homebrew, you may install it by entering the following command. 
      brew install pidgin
    • On a Debian or Ubuntu based system, you may install it by entering the following command. 
      apt-get update && apt-get install pidgin
  2. Run Pidgin.
  3. From the menu, select Accounts > Manage Accounts.
  4. Click Add.
  5. In the Basic tab, set the following details.
    • Protocol: IRC
    • Username: A nickname of your choice
    • Server: chat.freenode.net
    • Password: You may leave this blank if you are connecting for the first time. If you have registered your nickname before, enter your password here.
  6. Go to Advanced tab and set the following details.
    • Port: 6697
    • Select Use SSL.
  7. Click Add. Click Close.
  8. Wait for the status of Pidgin to change to Available with a green disc.
  9. From the menu, select Buddies > Join a Chat.. Enter the following details.
    • Account: Set it to the account that was just created.
    • Channel: #projectfermat
    • Password: Leave this blank. Our channel does not require password.
  10. Click Join.

As an alternative to the last two steps, if you find that new Freenode tabs open after the status changes to Available, you may simply enter the following command where you would normally type a chat message. 

/join #projectfermat

NickServ and ChanServ

There are two bots, NickServ and ChanServ, which help us with nickname and channel management activities. This is explained in the sections below.

Register Nickname

Once you have connected to the network, you can register your nickname. Traditionally, there was no concept of nickname registration in IRC. Anyone was free to use any unused nickname. But nowadays, in many IRC networks including Freenode, you can register a nickname to ensure that someone else cannot use your nickname.

To register the nickname, enter the following command on the IRC client. Remember to enter the password you want and your actual email address as the last two arguments in the command below. 

/msg nickserv register password email

You would receive an email with further instructions about completing the registration. Follow these instructions and complete the registration.

After you have registered, you can identify yourself to the network while connecting by entering your password in the Password field.

To identify yourself to the network while you are connected without reconnecting, enter the following command. 

/msg nickserv identify password

Note that simply registering your nickname doesn't prevent anyone else from using your nick when you are absent. It only ensures that you can recover your nick when you are online even if someone else is using it.

If you really want to ensure that nobody else is able to use your nick even when you are absent, enter the following command. 

/msg nickserv set enforce on

This ensures that even if someone uses your nick when you are absent, he is forced to change his nick within 30 seconds.

Recover Nickname

There are usually two situations in which you would want to recover your nick.

  1. Someone else is using your nick.
  2. You got disconnected from the network abruptly due to network issues but when you try to connect again, the server tells you that the nick is already being used. This happens when the server doesn't realize that your connection was terminated due to network issues. This is called a ghost connection. The server automatically terminates the ghost connection after a timeout of about 4.5 minutes.

To recover your nick, enter the following command. 

/msg nickserv ghost nick password

After executing that the server will tell you that the nickname has been ghosted. Now, you can change acquire your actual nick again with the command: /nick nick.

User Cheat Sheet

All IRC commands begin with a forward slash, i.e. /. Here is a list of some useful commands.

  1. Change nickname. 
    /nick newnick
  2. Join a channel. 
    /join channelname
  3. Find information about another user. 
    /whois nick
/msg nickserv info nick
  4. Recover your nick. 
    /msg nickserv ghost nick password
  5. Register your nick. 
    /msg nickserv register password email
  6. Identify yourself to the network. 
    /msg nickserv identify password

Operator Cheat Sheet

This section is relevant only to a channel operator. Founder of a channel, its operators or Freenode staff can add users as new operators of a channel. Here is a list of commands that are useful to a channel operator.

  1. Become an operator. 
    /msg chanserv op #projectfermat
  2. Become a normal user again with any one of the following commands. It is good etiquette to deop yourself when you don't need the operator privilege. 
    /msg chanserv deop #projectfermat
/deop #projectfermat
/mode -o #projectfermat
  3. Change topic of a channel. You may run the first command as a normal user as long as you have operator access for the channel. You may be required to become an operator before running the second command. 
    /msg chanserv topic #projectfermat New topic
/topic New topic
  4. Mute a troll based on nickname. 
    /mode +q nick
  5. Unmute a user based on nickname. 
    /mode -q nick
  6. Mute a troll based on hostmask. Sometimes a troll may reconnect with different nicks and spam the channel. In such a case, the troll may be muted based on their hostmask. 
    /mode +q *!*@hostmask
  7. Find the hostmask of a nickname. 
    /whois nick
    Look for a pattern that looks like username@hostmask in the output. The hostmask part may be a domain name, an IP address or a cloak. This part may be used to mute a troll in the command described in the previous point.
  8. The following ChanServ commands automatically figures out the hostmask of a nick and mutes or unmutes a nick based on its hostmask. 
    /msg chanserv quiet #projectfermat nick
/msg chanserv unquiet #projectfermat nick
  9. Ban a troll, i.e. mute the troll and prevent the troll from joining the channel again. 
    /mode +b nick
  10. Unban a user. 
    /mode -b nick
  11. Kick a troll out of the channel. 
    /kick nick

Let us see a few examples of how to mute a troll based on hostname or cloak.

  • Mute the hostname in ~chatzilla@tuwien.teleweb.at. 
    /mode +q *!*@tuwien.teleweb.at
  • Mute the IP address in ~zumbi@77.230.237.25. 
    /mode +q *!*@77.230.237.25
  • Mute the cloak in ~humpty@unaffiliated/humpty. 
    /mode +q *!*@unaffiliated/humpty
